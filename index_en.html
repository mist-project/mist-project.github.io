What is Mist

Mist is a powerful image preprocessing tool designed for the purpose of protecting the style and content of images from being mimicked by state-of-the-art AI-for-Art applications, including Stable Diffusion, NovelAI, and scenario.gg. By adding watermarks to the images, Mist renders them unrecognizable and inimitable for the models employed by AI-for-Art applications. Attempts by AI-for-Art applications to mimic these Misted images will be ineffective, and the output image of such mimicry will be scrambled and unusable as artwork.

Advantages & Examples

Mist is strong against various AI-for-Art applications, as it is robust to noise purification and time-efficient. The examples below demonstrate that Mist provides state-of-the-art protecting watermarks for images, with robustness against various noise purification methods, such as screenshotting, cropping and resizing. Furthermore, the watermarking process takes only several minutes.

Time efficiency close

The watermarking process is fast. With the default parameters (100 steps, 512 output size), Mist can process a image within 3 minutes.

Example: Mist adds watermarks to Monet's painting within 3 minutes. (GPU: RTX A4000)




Download

Mist can be downloaded by two approaches. For users, you can download the file from Huggingface. After unzipping the file, Mist can be launched by clicking the executable file “Mist.” For developers interested in exploring the implementation and other technical details of Mist, you may download the source code from GitHub and refer to the Readme file for installation instructions. Currently, Mist is compatible with both Windows and Linux operating systems.

Validate

Users can validate the effectiveness of Mist in two steps. First, add watermarks to images using Mist, Second, submit these Misted images to AI-for-Art applications, which will synthesize new images using their mimicry functions, for example, textual inversion and Dreambooth. Users can verify the protection strength of Mist by comparing the extent to which images are mimicked by AI-for-Art applications before and after being Misted. Detailed guidelines for the validation process can be found in our documentation.

FAQ close

Q: What is the computing resource requirement to run Mist?
A: We provide a table in our documentation that outlines the device requirement for running Mist. Additionally, for users with limited computing resources, we strongly recommend using Mist’s low VRAM mode, which will significantly reduce the computing resource requirement.

Q: What should I do if my device cannot run Mist even under the low VRAM mode?
A: We are aware of the access limitation due to device requirements and are actively working on a solution. In the near future, we plan to release Mist as an open-source serverless online service, which will allow all users to use Mist regardless of their device’s capabilities. We appreciate your patience and encourage you to stay tuned for updates on this exciting development.

Q: If I use an attack with a strength lower than recommended to make the watermark less obvious, would Mist still work?
A: Indeed, the recommended strength for Mist is designed to ensure optimal and comprehensive performance under different mimicry cases (textual inversion and Dreambooth in Stable Diffusion, Img2Img in NovelAI, etc.) If the user decides to reduce the watermark strength, Mist can still provide protection with relatively moderate performance. Additionally, Mist provides users with the option to choose from different modes that specifically target different mimicry cases, allowing users to adjust the watermark strength to better suit their needs. Detailed instructions on how to select the appropriate mode are provided in our documentation.

Q: Can Mist completely protect the image from being mimicked?
A: While we have thoroughly tested and validated Mist’s performance against Stable Diffusion, NovelAI, and scenario.gg, which currently represent the mainstream AI-for-Art applications with mimicry function, it is important to note that the effectiveness of Mist is not immune to future risks due to rapid development in this field. New challenges may arise from newly released AI-for-Art applications, novel mimicry functions, and new methods of watermark purification. We are committed to continuously improving Mist and warmly welcome user feedback and bug reports through our Discord community, which is open to both developers and users. Join us and help us stay ahead of the curve!
